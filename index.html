1. Scope of Services

readHD is a local-first application designed to enhance reading comprehension and focus using visual, cognitive, and sensory aids. The App operates primarily offline and does not maintain central user accounts or cloud storage servers. The App provides the following reading assistance techniques:

  • Local Documents Upload: Allows you to import EPUB, PDF, DOCX, and TXT files directly to your secure, offline container.
  • Fixation & Anchoring: Programmatically bolds the initial characters of each word (Prefix Bold) to establish optimal visual fixation points.
  • Spotlight Reading Ruler: A horizontal reading guide overlay that snaps to active lines and intelligently skips blank spacing.
  • Autopaced A/V Sync: Matches text highlights with speech engine pace or silent timers, with options to hide inactive words (Ghost Mode).
  • Color-Coded Highlighting: Supports selecting and color-highlighting text to generate summaries and flashcards locally.
  • RSVP Speed Presentation: Flashes words sequentially at custom speeds with a colored Optimal Recognition Point (ORP) letter.
  • Continuous Viewport Gradients: Renders static color gradient meshes behind text to aid eye-tracking and ease transition between lines.
  • Distraction-Free Brain Dump: A quick thoughtpad activated by shaking the device to journal distracting thoughts and return to reading.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 04, 2026

Your privacy is our absolute priority. This Privacy Policy describes how readHD handles your information.

1. Local-First Data Principle

readHD is built on a strict local-first architecture. This means:

  • No Personal Data Harvesting: We do not collect, capture, store, or transmit your custom nickname, bookmarks, highlighted text segments, ADHD brain dumps, or reading history.
  • Secure On-Device Storage: All your reading progress, imported books, notes, and local AI flashcards are saved strictly within the secure application sandbox container on your physical device.

2. Device Resource Permissions

To provide our cognitive reading features, the App requests access to:

  • Device Accelerometer: Used strictly on-device to recognize shake gestures to launch the ADHD Brain Dump interface. No motion sensor data is uploaded or logged.
  • System Text-to-Speech (TTS): Dictation features utilize your operating system's native voice synthesizer. All voice conversion occurs locally on-device.

3. Purchases & Anonymous Identifiers

We use RevenueCat to process premium subscriptions. Purchases are authenticated anonymously using a local randomly generated user ID. No real name, email, or billing address is sent to us.

4. Data Retention & Deletion

Because all data is stored on-device, we cannot access, retrieve, or restore your data. You can delete all your application data at any time by selecting the "DELETE ACCOUNT & DATA" option in the settings panel or by uninstalling the App.

5. Contact Information

For privacy inquiries, please reach out to us at privacy@readhd.app.

Account & Data Deletion

Last Updated: July 04, 2026
⚠

Critical Warning

This action is immediate and completely irreversible. All local data will be permanently lost.

Because readHD is a **local-first** application, we do not host your accounts or reading records on our servers. As a result, you do not need to contact us or wait for server-side processing to delete your data.

Option 1: In-App Purge (Recommended)

You can instantly delete all your preferences, nickname, highlights, brain dumps, and library books directly inside the App:

  1. Open the readHD App on your mobile device.
  2. Go to the Settings panel (accessible from the bottom dashboard tab).
  3. Scroll down to the Account Management section.
  4. Tap the red button labeled Delete Account & Data.
  5. Confirm the action in the prompt. All database slices and book files will be instantly wiped from the device sandbox.

Option 2: Delete by Uninstalling

Alternatively, uninstalling the application from your iOS or Android operating system will instruct the OS to automatically delete the application sandbox container, which contains all highlights, offline brain dumps, and downloaded books.
